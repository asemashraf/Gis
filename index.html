Natural Resource Management

Explanation: GIS technologies are used to monitor and assess natural resources such as water, forests, and mines. These systems help in mapping utilization areas, identifying optimal investment sites, and monitoring the sustainable use of resources.

Urban and Municipal Planning

Explanation: GIS is used in the design and implementation of urban development plans by analyzing the distribution of population, roads, and public facilities. It supports better management of population growth, traffic organization, and the planning of public services such as water and electricity.

Disaster and Emergency Management

Explanation: GIS is applied in tracking natural disasters such as earthquakes, floods, and storms. The system provides detailed hazard maps, helping emergency teams coordinate quickly and deliver aid to affected areas more efficiently..

Smart Agriculture

Explanation: In agriculture, GIS is used to analyze soil, monitor crops, and determine the best farming practices in specific regions. It helps farmers improve productivity, reduce waste, and manage resources effectively.
